# USER GUIDE: NAVIGATING THE COLLECTION

To effectively use this archive, visitors should utilize the **Browse** page for visual discovery and the **Search** function for thematic research. Every item is linked to a specific physical location in **Box 4 (Box 8107)** at Cornell University, allowing for a seamless transition between digital exploration and physical archival study.

## 1. The Archive and the Honors Program

The **Reagan-Bush Era Punk Poster Archive** is a digital humanities project developed and curated by **Catherine Frank** ('26) as part of the **Cornell University Honors Program**. The collection serves as a scholarly intervention into the preservation of ephemeral political art, ensuring that these DIY aesthetics remain available for future academic study.

## 2. Historical Context: Dissent in the 80s

The years 1977 to 1992 represent a transformative period in American political expression. As the Reagan and Bush administrations shaped domestic and foreign policy, the punk underground responded with a surge of "cut-and-paste" visual communication. This archive captures that friction, documenting how marginalized youth used the xerox machine as a tool for radical democratic participation.

## 3. Curator’s Statement

> "The goal of this project was to move these artifacts from the silence of an archival box into a dynamic digital environment. By mapping the visual language of the Reagan-Bush era, we can see how marginalized voices used limited resources to create a massive ideological impact."

## 4. Digital Preservation Goals

Beyond mere digitization, this project aims to create a "living archive". By providing high-resolution derivatives and open metadata, we empower researchers to perform their own computational analyses on the collection. Our goal is to democratize access to these fragile records before the original newsprint and tape further degrade.

## 5. Research Methodology

This project utilizes the **CollectionBuilder-CSV** framework to transform raw archival metadata into an interactive research tool. Unlike a simple gallery, this archive is built on **structured data**, mapping locations and subjects to reveal ideological networks.

## 6. About Catherine Frank

**Catherine Frank** is a senior at **Cornell University** graduating in 2026 with a double major in Government and Music. This archive represents the culmination of her honors work, bridging interests in archival science, history, and digital preservation.

## 7. Technical Note & Sources

The artifacts in this collection were processed from **Box 4 (Box 8107)** of the *Cometbus Punk and Underground Press Collection* at Cornell University. For inquiries regarding metadata or archival sources, please visit the [Data page]({{ "/data.html" | relative_url }}).
