## The Archive and the Honors Program

The **Reagan-Bush Era Punk Poster Archive** is a digital humanities project developed and curated by **Catherine Frank** ('26) as part of the **Cornell University Honors Program**. 

The collection serves as a scholarly intervention into the preservation of ephemeral political art. By digitizing over 432 unique posters, flyers, and zine fragments—originally produced on low-grade paper for immediate consumption—this project ensures that these DIY aesthetics remain available for academic study.

### **Curator's Statement**
> "The goal of this project was to move these artifacts from the silence of an archival box into a dynamic digital environment. By mapping the visual language of the Reagan-Bush era, we can see how marginalized voices used limited resources to create a massive ideological impact."

### **Research Methodology**
This project utilizes the **CollectionBuilder-CSV** framework to transform raw archival metadata into an interactive research tool. Unlike a simple gallery, this archive is built on **structured data**, mapping specific locations, subjects, and creators to reveal ideological networks often lost in traditional filing systems.

### **About Catherine Frank**
**Catherine Frank** is a senior at **Cornell University** graduating with a double major in Government and Music. This archive represents the culmination of her work within the honors program, bridging interests in archival science, history, and digital preservation. Following her graduation in 2026, she aims to continue exploring how digital tools can democratize access to marginalized records.
