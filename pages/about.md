## The Archive and the Honors Program

The **Reagan-Bush Era Punk Poster Archive** is a digital humanities project developed and curated by **Catherine Frank** ('26) as part of the **Cornell University Honors Program**. 

The collection serves as a scholarly intervention into the preservation of ephemeral political art. By digitizing over 432 unique posters, flyers, and zine fragments—many of which were originally produced on low-grade paper for immediate consumption—this project ensures that the "DIY" aesthetics of resistance from 1977 to 1992 are available for future academic study.

### **Curator's Statement**
> "The goal of this project was to move these artifacts from the silence of an archival box into a dynamic digital environment. By mapping the visual language of the Reagan-Bush era, we can see how marginalized voices used limited resources to create a massive ideological impact. This archive is a testament to the power of the xerox machine and the street pole as tools for political speech."

### **Collection Highlights & Scope**
The 432 items in this collection were selected to represent the diversity of the punk underground. The archive includes:
* **Political Flyers:** Direct responses to the Reagan and Bush administrations' domestic and foreign policies.
* **Show Posters:** Documents of the DIY music circuit that sustained these political subcultures.
* **Zine Fragments:** Snippets of underground press that provided the intellectual backbone of the movement.

### **Research Methodology & Information Architecture**
This project utilizes the **CollectionBuilder-CSV** framework to transform raw archival metadata into an interactive research tool. 

Unlike a simple image gallery, this archive is built on **structured data**. Every item has been meticulously cataloged with metadata—mapping specific locations, subjects, and creators. This architectural approach allows researchers to reveal geographic and ideological networks of the punk underground that are often lost in traditional physical filing systems.

### **About Catherine Frank**
**Catherine Frank** is a senior at **Cornell University** graduating with a double major in Government and Music. This archive represents the culmination of her work within the honors program, bridging her interests in archival science, history, and digital preservation. 

Following her graduation in 2026, Catherine aims to continue exploring how digital tools can be used to democratize access to marginalized historical records.
